Highlights places in which Lombok generates dangerous code for a JPA/Hibernate @Entity

Including all fields in a generated toString implementation can trigger lazy-loading collections of children leading to runtime exceptions or (worse) degraded performance caused by (sometime useless) network traffic and memory churn. @ToString.Exclude will be added to all collection fields, unless they were already marked with @ToString.Include

Implementing hashCode and equals on all fields of an @Entity is generally discouraged, as the ORM assigns the @Id and other fields at .persist() time. This will cause the hashCode to return a different value, which may lead to unexpected behavior when adding your instances of your Entities of a HashSet or as keys in a HashMap.

Lastly, from a design point of view, opening all fields to read/write from outside breaks encapsulation of your Domain Entity, and promotes an Anemic Domain Model. In general, it is advisable to only provide setters for those fields that can freely be changed from outside. In short, the decision to break encapsulation should be made with greater care in most projects.
